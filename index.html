CT Calibration

Uncalibrated CT system schema

In this paper, a set of CT calibration algorithm is designed according to the given CT calibration template, which has better accuracy and stability. The reconstruction model and algorithm of the reverse absorption rate through the projection value are established. After denoising, better image of absorption can be acquired. At the same time, a new CT calibration template was designed to improve the performance of the calibration algorithm by making full use of the geometric features.

NLOS Imaging

Schematic diagram of the experiment setup.

We derived a probability based model for solving NLOS problem to handle random error in data acquisition and reflective characteristics of reflectors and proposed a new algorithm and sampling method. We also validated its applicability on simulated and experimental data. The result shows good image quality and higher efficiency in both memory and time than conventional BP methods. This algorithm can also be applied to retroreflective objects and confocal sampling method.

Bessel Beam

Cross section of generated Bessel beam.

We use a single phase-only spatial light modulator (SLM) to control the axial distribution of Bessel beams, making it approximate to arbitrary given distribution curve. Given the expected axial distribution and illuminated intensity on SLM plane, we are able to conduct phase retrieval algorithm to realize a complex field on its back focal plane, which is similar to the expected Bessel beam distribution in signal region (SR). After filtering out the noise region (NR) on the back focal plane, one can get the expected Bessel beam with approximating axial intensity distribution.

This method is prospective in that it can control the peak intensity of two light at different wavelengths in SRS system to overlap with each other, and able to transmit more energy into deeper part of the brain in neuron stimulation experiments.

OCT Angiography

Auto-classification result of phantom data.

We propose a complex-correlation based statistical model to depict different types of pixels in OCT angiography (OCTA) images. Besides, unsupervised learning algorithm is applied to classify regions into groups automatically. Basically, all pixels are classified into three groups, static, flowing and noise.

The method demonstrates effectiveness and great performance on the phantom data. Different groups are colored in three colors and can be distinguished clearly. It is relatively innovative in that this method automatically extracts and enhances blood vessels in the OCT image with high precision and resolution.
